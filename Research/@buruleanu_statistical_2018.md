abstract: ABSTRACTThe antioxidant activity, total phenolic content and total flavonoid content of ten edible mushrooms species, including cultivated (Pleurotus ostreatus, Agaricus bisporus white and brown) and wild (Pleurotus ostreatus, Macrolepiota procera, Cantharellus cibarius, Russula vesca, Russula alutacea, Boletus edulis and Agaricus campestris), were determined. The extraction was performed using water and 50% water – ethanol and the caps and stipe were investigated separately. Water was the most appropriate solvent for phenolic compounds regardless of the sample. On the contrary, the presence of ethanol in the solvent increased the extraction of flavonoids for cultivated Pleurotus ostreatus (caps and stipe), and wild Russula alutacea, Russula vesca, Agaricus campestris, Pleurotus ostreatus (only caps) and Cantharellus cibarius, Macrolepiota procera (only stipe).
